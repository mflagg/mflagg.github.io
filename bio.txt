Matt Flagg received his Ph.D. in Computer Science from Georgia Tech in 2010, specializing in computer vision, graphics and human-computer interaction. His tracking research earned the Best Student Paper award at the British Machine Vision Conference and a Foley Scholars Award, and his peer-reviewed work — spanning projector-camera systems, motion-coherent tracking and video synthesis — has been cited more than 1,100 times in venues including IJCV, CVPR and ACM UIST.

Before consulting, he co-founded PlayMotion, a gesture-recognition company that deployed hundreds of projector-camera systems across hospitals, museums and family entertainment centers, including three divisions of Disney parks. He also contributed to a 3D scanning system honored with a Scientific and Engineering Award from the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences at Industrial Light & Magic. That early work grew into a portfolio of more than ten U.S. patents covering gesture control, interactive displays, virtual try-on, active lighting and identity-document security.

Over the past decade Flagg has researched and developed perception systems for more than fifteen clients, from Meta Reality Labs to venture capital investors. His engagements span the full breadth of visual computing — LiDAR-based opponent detection for autonomous racecars, real-time markerless motion capture of professional soccer matches, document authentication and biometric fraud detection, 3D facial scanning for cosmetic pre-consultation and custom eyewear, photo-to-avatar synthesis that helped launch AR Emoji on Samsung devices, and technical due diligence of computer-vision investments.

Today Flagg is Chief Science Officer at Code19 Racing — the world's first professional autonomous motorsports team — and leads Flagg Visual Computing, a collective that pairs him with a deep bench of longtime engineers and researchers. He collaborates with longtime partner Dr. Satya Mallick (CEO of OpenCV), embedding with each client's team to design, prototype and ship perception systems end to end. All client IP is owned by the customer.
